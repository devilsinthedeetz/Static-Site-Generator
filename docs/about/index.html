So, About Me...

Hi! I'm Andrew, a 30 something year old man from Utah. I've lived here in the salt lake valley all of my life, and I honestly love it here. It's great to be able to drive for 30 minutes and suddenly find myself on a gorgeous mountain trail. An escape from the urban hellscape is never too far away.

I'm currently in my last semester of obtaining my BSN Degree to further my career in Nursing. I have been a healthcare professional since 2017, starting out as a Critical Care Tech in the Respiratory ICU at trauma 1 hospital in Salt lake. These days I'm an ICU nurse at a Medical-Surgical ICU. It's not at the same trauma 1 hospital I was working at, but we aren't some po-dunk community hospital that sends out their sick either (if you know what an APACHE score is, our patient's score sometimes rival the trauma 1's). Unless they need specialty care such as neuro-surgery, or a heart cath, we're keeping them. We are also an "Awake and Walking" ICU, which means we keep our intubated patients off sedation and get them MOVING. It turns out our mothers were right: staying in bed all day isn't good for you and if you don't use it you'll lose it (muscle mass that is). I get the privilege to see the patients I take care of walk out of the ICU and rarely ever have to be transferred to a rehab facility. It's safe to say I'm proud of working here. But I don't see myself doing this forever. I hope to grow into a career in either nursing informatics...

Or maybe just straight up tech? Who knows. What I do know is that while I am away from work I cosplay as a sysadmin (I have a sloppy home lab),a software engineer (I wrote some clever bash scripts and am halfway through boot.dev), and a hybrid athlete (I am slower than a highschool track athlete). Outside of work and tech hobbies, I'm a bit of a fitness nerd. Lifting heavy weights, running (road and trail), and swimming are usually my go to activities. For me, testing my physical limits is a thrill, and training hard is my way of taking care of both my body and mind (especially when I'm dipping my toes into mild rhabdo and ATN on top of Mt Baldy 23 miles into Speedgoat). The balancing act of training with full-time work and school (and hobbies) is all part of the challenge and the journey.
